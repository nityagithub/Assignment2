The Australian Bushfires

Distribution of Bushfires across Australia

Historically, Australian Bushfires have been immensely difficult to control and destroyed various communities and towns. More than 10 million hectares of land was destroyed, burning through vegetation, wildlife, homes and killing many residents. Aqua and Terra Satellites by Nasa present variables correlated with bushfires, particularly the Fire Radiation Point and Brightness. Further studies of climate conditions of different regions prove to be stimulants of this natural disaster.

This presents the distribution of fires across Australia with their respective Brightness Temperature and Fire Radiation Power in each area. Predominantly, the bushfires are distributed along the coastline of Victoria and New South Wales. However, clusters do appear in the Northern and Southern regions of Australia.

Fire Prone Areas (At a glance)

Some of the regions most affected by Australian bushfires include; Bairnsdale East, Bendigo, Esperance, Kalgoorie, Kangaroo Island and Mildura. Different climate conditions can prove to be more or less vulnerable to bushfires

An Australian Weather Dataset focusing on the respective areas is used for intricate analysis to determine whether a correlation exists between climate variables and prevalence of bushfires. NASA Satellites - Aqua and Terra

Bushfire and Weather Data is captured through the NASA satellites. Referring to a NASA published article, both Aqua and Terra satellites utilise Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) to derive mathematical calculations relating to bushfires in Australia. The level of detection of brightness is prominent in higher magnitudes by the Aqua satellite relative to the Terra satellite.The level of confidence is indicative of the reliability of the values provided by NASA and hence, has been placed in parralel with the heatmap.

Credibility of NASA Satellites

The measurements observed by Aqua and Terra Satellite is combined with a confidence level which indicates the precision and certainty of the numerical values of the Fire Radiation Power and Brightness included within the dataset. Safety and support

Bushfires have been everpresent in Australia and the effects are brutal. There are physical and direct impacts, ecological impacts and economic costs associated with this natural disasters. The distribution of fires across Australia due to its climate will continue to make several towns vulnerable.
